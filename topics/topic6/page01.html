Manual Handling

What is manual handling?

Many people would think that manual handling only refers to lifting and carrying something. In fact, the term includes lifting, lowering, pushing, pulling, moving, holding or restraining an object. It also covers activities that require the use of force or effort such as pulling a lever or operating power tools. Following are some important things to remember.

Items to be handled may be:

  • Awkward in shape
  • Large or very small
  • Hard to reach
  • Located at a different height – either too low or too high
  • Heavy
  • Moving
  • Potentially hazardous

Reduce manual handling injuries by:

  • Practice safe lifting techniques
  • Lighten loads (break loads into smaller quantities)
  • Reduce bending, twisting, reaching movements
  • Use team lifting
  • Use mechanical assistance (e.g. trolleys and adjustable height workbenches and seating)
  • Prevent muscle strain and fatigue. This includes warming up before working, taking rest breaks, and allowing time to get used to a new task

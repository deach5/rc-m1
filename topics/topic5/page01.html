It is essential that if you are exposed to chemicals or hazardous substances at your workplace, you are trained and aware of any Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) that you need, to ensure your safety when handling dangerous goods.

For example, wearing gloves and overalls so that your skin doesn’t contact the goods. Your employee has a legal obligation to provide protective equipment or clothing and to maintain it in good order as part of the risk control process.

Employees, employers and PPE

Click each image below to learn more about Employees, Employers and PPE.

Employees and PPE

Employees must use PPE and clothing provided by the employer. They should know why and how to use it before they start any nominated job.

Employer choosing PPE

Employers should make sure that the PPE they purchase for their employees comply with the relevant Australian Standard.

Employer storing PPE

Employers must make sure that PPE is stored in a clean and fully operational condition, that it can be easily found when needed and that it is safe from interference and damage.

Note. PPE should be inspected regularly and before use to make sure it is in good working order. Damaged or defective PPE must be reported at once. It must be tagged (to prevent its use) until it has been repaired, thrown away or replaced.
